Introduction to DAA

Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DAA) is a core subject in computer science that focuses on designing efficient algorithms and evaluating their performance. It helps students understand how to optimize solutions using concepts such as Divide & Conquer, Greedy strategies, Dynamic Programming, Graph algorithms, and more. These principles guide us in building systems that are fast, scalable, and efficient.

How DAA is Used in a City Design Project

City design requires solving real-world optimization problems such as traffic routing, shortest path planning, resource distribution, facility placement, and network connectivity. Using DAA concepts, we can:

✔ Use **Graph Algorithms** to design road networks and shortest paths.
✔ Apply **Greedy Algorithms** for optimal placement of hospitals, fire stations, and parks.
✔ Use **Dynamic Programming** to minimize construction cost or space usage.
✔ Use **Minimum Spanning Tree (MST)** techniques for connecting city zones efficiently.
✔ Use **Search Algorithms** to plan traffic movement and vehicle routing.

By combining algorithmic thinking with smart city planning, we develop a more structured, optimized, and scalable city layout.
