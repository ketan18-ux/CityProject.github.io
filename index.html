What is the course about?

Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DAA) focuses on how to design efficient algorithms and analyse their performance. It forms the logical backbone of computer science, helping us understand how fast, optimal and scalable our solutions are for real-world problems.

What kind of data structures and algorithms have you studied?

We have studied arrays, linked lists, stacks, queues, trees, graphs and hashing. On the algorithm side, we explored searching, sorting, divide-and-conquer, greedy algorithms, dynamic programming, backtracking and shortest-path algorithms. These concepts help us make our city design more structured and efficient.

How do you connect the course with real-time applications?

Algorithms power navigation systems, traffic control, resource allocation and smart grid planning. In our City Design project, we use these ideas to simulate better road networks, reduce congestion and plan zones logically. DAA helps us convert raw data into meaningful models for a smarter and scalable city.
