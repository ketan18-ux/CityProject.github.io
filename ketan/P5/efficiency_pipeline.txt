Efficiency Analysis — Water Pipeline Pressure Optimization (Problem 5)

Goal:
Rank pipes based on downstream demand and pipe capacity using:
1. DFS to compute subtree demand.
2. Quick Sort to rank pipes by score = downstreamDemand / capacity.

Definitions:
n = number of nodes (20 in dataset)
m = number of pipes (20 in dataset)

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 1 — DFS to Compute Downstream Demand
------------------------------------------------------------
The DFS traverses each node and each pipe exactly once.
Adjacency list representation is used.

Time:
    O(n + m)

Space:
    subtreeDemand array: O(n)
    recursion stack: O(n)
    adjacency list: O(n + m)

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 2 — Compute Pipe Score
------------------------------------------------------------
For each pipe (u, v):
- find the higher subtree demand
- compute score = demand / capacity

Cost:
    O(m)

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 3 — Quick Sort of Pipes by Score
------------------------------------------------------------
Quick sort average case: O(m log m)
Worst case (rare): O(m²)

Since m = 20, sorting is extremely fast.

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 4 — Overall Time Complexity
------------------------------------------------------------
DFS:            O(n + m)
Score compute:  O(m)
Sorting:        O(m log m)

Total:
    O(n + m log m)

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 5 — Space Complexity
------------------------------------------------------------
Adjacency list:     O(n + m)
Demand arrays:      O(n)
Pipes array:        O(m)

Total:
    O(n + m)

------------------------------------------------------------
Conclusion:
The algorithm is efficient for smart-city water networks. 
Even for large networks with thousands of pipes, 
O(n + m log m) is scalable and suitable for real-time monitoring.
