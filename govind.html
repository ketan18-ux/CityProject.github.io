Resource, Budget & Revenue Optimization Projects

Solutions implemented using foundational CS algorithms and data structures taught in class.

1. Best Allocation of City Budget

Algorithm: 0/1 Knapsack (DP)

Projects are items with cost and benefit; dynamic programming selects the optimal subset under budget.

2. Tax Officer Route Optimization

Algorithm: Floyd–Warshall (all-pairs shortest paths) + Backtracking

Compute shortest paths between all locations, then backtrack to construct near-optimal visit order minimizing total distance.

3. Smart Parking Fee Prediction

Algorithm: Segment tree (range aggregation) + Lookup table

Use segment trees to aggregate demand over time windows and a lookup table to map demand bands to optimal fees.

4. City Energy Demand Forecasting

Algorithm: Fenwick tree (prefix sums) + Recurrence

Maintain rolling aggregates with Fenwick tree; apply a simple recurrence on recent windows to forecast next-period demand.

5. Metro Construction Phase Optimization

Algorithm: Max-heap (priority by ROI) + Greedy selection

Push phases keyed by ROI into a max-heap and pick in order until budget is exhausted for maximum cumulative ROI.

6. Streetlight Energy Reduction

Algorithm: Interval scheduling with segment tree

Model dimming windows as intervals; use a segment tree to manage overlaps and enforce optimal non-conflicting schedules.

7. Transport Ticket Dynamic Pricing

Algorithm: AVL tree (price bands) + Heap (demand peaks)

Maintain price bands in an AVL tree for fast updates; use a max-heap of demand peaks to raise/lower prices efficiently.

8. Garbage Plant Load Balancing

Algorithm: Min-heap (least loaded server) + Queue simulation

Simulate waste arrivals; assign each batch to the plant with minimal current load tracked by a min-heap to balance throughput.

9. Building Maintenance Prediction

Algorithm: 2–3 tree (time-indexed events) + Recursion

Index maintenance logs in a 2–3 tree for balanced queries and recursively analyze patterns to predict upcoming service needs.

10. Subsidy Distribution Optimization

Algorithm: Quick sort (benefit/cost ratio) + Greedy

Sort beneficiaries by benefit-to-cost ratio using quick sort; greedily select until budget cap to maximize total welfare impact.
