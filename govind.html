Algorithmic Solutions to City Problems

Applying core CS algorithms (recursion, trees, sorting, graphs, string search) to real-world optimization.

1. Best Allocation of City Budget

Algorithm: 0/1 Knapsack (DP)

Dynamic programming ensures maximum benefit under fixed budget constraints.

2. Tax Officer Route Optimization

Algorithm: Dijkstra / Bellman-Ford

Shortest path algorithms compute minimum travel routes for inspection officers.

3. Smart Parking Fee Prediction

Algorithm: Regression + Lookup Table

Lookup tables and regression models predict optimal fees based on usage history.

4. City Energy Demand Forecasting

Algorithm: Recursion + Segment Tree

Segment trees efficiently forecast demand by aggregating time-series data.

5. Metro Construction Phase Optimization

Algorithm: Greedy + Heap

Heap-based greedy selection prioritizes construction phases with highest ROI.

6. Streetlight Energy Reduction

Algorithm: Scheduling + AVL Tree

AVL trees maintain balanced schedules for dynamic dimming and energy savings.

7. Transport Ticket Dynamic Pricing

Algorithm: Heap Sort + Fenwick Tree

Heap sort ranks demand levels; Fenwick tree updates pricing dynamically.

8. Garbage Plant Load Balancing

Algorithm: Queue Simulation + Skip List

Skip lists and queues distribute waste loads evenly across plants.

9. Building Maintenance Prediction

Algorithm: Divide & Conquer + Trie

Tries store historical maintenance patterns; divide & conquer predicts future needs.

10. Subsidy Distribution Optimization

Algorithm: Kruskal / Prim + N-Queens Backtracking

Graph MST algorithms ensure fair distribution; backtracking handles complex beneficiary constraints.
